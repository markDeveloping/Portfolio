Introduction

The Government Digital Strategy commits government to ensuring all new or redesigned digital services meet a defined standard from April 2014.

This requires departments to demonstrate they have met the Digital by Default criteria defined below and that they have the capability to be able to maintain this quality for the full life of their services

Background

To establish whether the CS Resourcing eRecruitment services are required to meet the standard the following questions must be considered:

  • Is the service completely new and/or being redesigned?
  • Will the service process (or be likely to process) more than 100,000 transactions every year?
  • Is the service the responsibility of a central government department, agency or non-departmental public body?
  • Is the service being launched in April 2014 or after?

If the answer to these questions is Yes then the service must meet the standard before it can go live. If the answer is No to one or more of these questions then the service does not have to meet the standard.

Whilst the answer to the first 3 questions above is ‘Yes’ the answer to the final bullet is strictly ‘No’ as the new eRecruitment system is due to go live in January 2014. However, it is proposed that in designing the new system CS Resourcing seeks to meet the standard for the following reasons:

  • In discussions with Cabinet Office earlier in the year it was made clear that there is an expectation that CS Resourcing will meet the standard
  • The standard adopts best practice guidelines which CS Resourcing should be following whether they are mandated or not.

Summary

CS Resourcing believes that in building and implementing the new system it will be fully compliant with all 26 criteria of the Digital by Default Standard as set out in Annex A.
